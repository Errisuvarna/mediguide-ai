# Hospital Admission Guide

## Overview
This guide explains the step-by-step admission process for patients being admitted for treatment, surgery, or observation.

## Step 1: Doctor's Advice
Admission begins after a consulting doctor recommends it, either from the OPD or the Emergency Department. The doctor issues an admission note specifying the department and expected duration of stay.

## Step 2: Admission Counter
Go to the Admission counter on the Ground Floor of the Main Building. Present the admission note along with your ID proof and insurance card (if applicable).

## Step 3: Deposit
An admission deposit is required before a bed is allotted, unless the case is a medical emergency. The amount depends on the department and expected procedure; the Admission counter staff will provide an estimate.

## Step 4: Bed Allotment
Based on availability and the doctor's recommendation, you will be allotted a bed in a General Ward, Semi-Private Room, Private Room, or ICU.

## Step 5: Documents Required
- Government ID Proof
- Insurance Card / Policy Document (if applicable)
- Admission Deposit Receipt
- Previous Medical Records (if any)
- Emergency Contact Details Form

## Discharge Process
On the day of discharge, the doctor issues a discharge summary, the Billing counter settles the final bill, and the nursing station completes the paperwork before you can leave the ward.
